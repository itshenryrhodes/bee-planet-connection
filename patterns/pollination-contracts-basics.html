Pollination Contracts: The Essentials

Clear contracts protect both grower and beekeeper. They set expectations on hive numbers, timing, site access, and paymentâ€”reducing friction during a critical window.

What a good contract covers

  • Scope: crop, blocks, and dates for placement and removal.
  • Hive strength: frames of bees/brood at delivery and verification method.
  • Placement & access: vehicle access, stand locations, water provision.
  • Risk & liability: pesticide use, spray windows, notifications. See Pesticide Safety in Pollination.
  • Payment terms: rate, deposit, inspection, non-performance clauses.

Before you sign

  • Walk the site; check forage edges, wind exposure, and water.
  • Align hive numbers with bloom density and field layout.
  • Agree comms protocol for weather delays and spray alerts.

Proof points

Photographs at delivery and a simple inspection sheet keep everyone honest without introducing friction. See Inspections and Records and Invoices & Records.

Insurance & compliance

Confirm public liability, vehicle cover, and food-handling compliance if on-site extraction is planned. See Pollination Service Insurance.
