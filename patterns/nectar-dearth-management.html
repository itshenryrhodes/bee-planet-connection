Nectar Dearth Management

During a dearth, colonies become edgy, robbing risks rise, and brood areas contract. Small, steady interventions keep everything calm until forage returns.

Spotting a dearth early

  • Foragers drift and search; washboard behaviour increases.
  • Hive weights flatten or fall; nectar drying slows.

Stabilise the colony

  • Reduce entrances and tidy yard spillages.
  • Avoid tearing open nectar frames; work quickly and late in the day.
  • Consider light feeding if brood is at risk. See Feeding Strategies.

Prevent robbing

Fit screens on vulnerable colonies and keep equipment covered. See Robbing Prevention.

Plan for the next gap

Plant drought-tolerant forage and staggered bloom near the apiary. See Planting Guides and Ivy as Autumn Forage.
