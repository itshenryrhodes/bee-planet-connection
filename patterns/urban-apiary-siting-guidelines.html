Urban Apiary Siting Guidelines

Cities can be excellent for bees if sites are chosen with neighbours and forage in mind. Good siting avoids complaints and keeps colonies stable.

Choosing a spot

  • Hidden flight paths above head height; plant screens where possible.
  • Solid stands, level ground, and secure access.
  • Reliable water on site. See Water Sources for Bees.

Neighbour relations

  • Share a contact number and inspection schedule.
  • Manage swarms proactively; place bait hives in spring. See Bait Hives and Swarm Lures.

Local rules

Check council by-laws and tenancy agreements; some require set-backs or limits on hive numbers. Keep entrances away from paths and play areas.
