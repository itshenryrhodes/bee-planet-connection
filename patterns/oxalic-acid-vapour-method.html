Oxalic Acid Vapour Method

Oxalic acid vapour (OAV) is a reliable, fast treatment for phoretic Varroa destructor, best used when brood is minimal. It’s popular for winter tidy-ups and gaps between brood cycles.

When to use it

  • Mid-winter or brood-light periods for maximum effect.
  • After queen caging or brood breaks in summer IPM programmes. See Varroa & IPM.

Preparation

  • Seal obvious gaps to keep vapour in the hive for several minutes.
  • Use a calibrated vaporiser and dry oxalic acid dihydrate.
  • PPE: eye protection and a respirator suitable for acid vapours.

Application steps

  1. Insert vaporiser through entrance or an access hole; place dose on the pan.
  2. Power on for the manufacturer-specified time until sublimation completes.
  3. Leave the hive closed 10 minutes to let crystals settle.

Dosing and repeats

Follow national guidance and device instructions. In brood-right periods, repeat treatments are needed to catch mites as they emerge. See Treatment Options and Thymol Guide for rotation planning.

Common pitfalls

  • Treating heavy brood nests once and expecting full control.
  • Poor sealing, resulting in low deposit on bees.
  • Skipping PPE; vapour is hazardous—treat downwind and avoid enclosed spaces.

Aftercare & monitoring

Confirm efficacy with a follow-up mite count using your preferred method. See Varroa Monitoring Methods.
