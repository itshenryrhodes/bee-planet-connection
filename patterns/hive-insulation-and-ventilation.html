Principles

  • Warmth matters to brood; moisture management matters to the whole box.
  • Insulation reduces energy load; ventilation removes damp, not heat per se.
  • Local climate dictates your balance; copy successful neighbours.

Common setups

  • Insulated crownboard/roof: reduces winter condensation.
  • Top vents or quilts: absorb moisture and allow slow release. See Ventilation Shims and Quilts.
  • Open mesh floor: helpful for ventilation and mite drop checks.

Seasonal adjustments

  • Winter: close large vents, add insulation; keep a small top outlet for damp air.
  • Summer: ensure shade in heatwaves and avoid roof metal in full sun. See Heatwaves and Cooling.

Tell-tales of trouble

  • Water streaks under the roof; black mould on top bars.
  • Bearding every warm evening suggests heat build-up or space shortage. See Supering Strategies.
