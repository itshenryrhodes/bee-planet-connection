Record Keeping for Beekeepers

Short, consistent notes turn instincts into decisions. They also de-stress inspections and reveal patterns youâ€™d otherwise miss.

What to record

  • Queen status, brood stage, temperament, and space needs.
  • Mite counts and disease signs. See Varroa Monitoring Methods.
  • Forage notes, weather, and weight changes.

Keep it lightweight

A pre-printed sheet or simple app with checkboxes beats long prose. The goal is comparability across weeks and colonies.

Why it pays off

  • Improves timing for supering and swarm control. See Supering Strategies and Swarm Control.
  • Supports IPM planning and treatment rotation. See IPM for Bees.

Archiving

Keep one season folder per apiary; a single photo of the brood pattern each visit complements written notes beautifully.
