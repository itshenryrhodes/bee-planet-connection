Honey Harvest and Extraction Basics

A clean, calm harvest protects honey quality and keeps colonies settled. Most of it is preparation.

Before you lift a super

  • Only take frames ≥ 80% capped, or confirm moisture with a refractometer. See Moisture & Refractometers.
  • Clear bees with escape boards or gentle brushing; avoid fume boards near public.
  • Stage food-safe tubs and lids; keep the route from apiary to room short.

In the room

  • Uncap cleanly; keep knives warm, not hot.
  • Spin at modest speed; avoid aeration and foaming.
  • Strain as needed; don’t over-filter if selling as raw honey.

Quality & compliance

Mind hygiene, traceability, and accurate labelling. See Food Safety for Honey Packers and Grading & Standards.

After the harvest

Let bees clean wet supers briefly, then store dry to prevent wax moth. See Comb Storage.
