Small Hive Beetle: What to Watch For

Aethina tumida damages colonies by slime-outs and store spoilage. Good apiary hygiene and strong colonies are your best defences.

Identification & signs

  • Adults: small, dark beetles scuttling across frames and under lids.
  • Larvae: cream, segmented, often massed in comb with wet, fermenting honey.
  • Damage: slimed frames, sour smell, bees abandoning contaminated comb.

Risk factors

  • Warm, humid sites; shaded, damp apiaries.
  • Weak colonies and excessive space; honey houses with poor sanitation.

Prevention first

  • Keep colonies appropriately sized for bee coverage.
  • Remove burr comb and scraps; extract supers promptly.
  • Use ground sanitation around stands; avoid long-term pallet storage.

Control options

Traps and absorptive pads help knock back adult numbers; aggressive sanitation breaks breeding. If infestation is severe, isolate and salvage uncontaminated frames, freezing what you can. See Comb Storage for handling vulnerable equipment.

What not to do

Avoid leaving wet supers lying about or stacking cappings warm; these are magnets for beetles. Keep honey rooms dry and clean.
