What is Apiculture?

Apiculture is the care and management of managed bees — most commonly honey bees — for honey, pollination, education, and research.

The short version

Apiculture (beekeeping) brings people and bees together. Beekeepers provide suitable hives, nutrition, and health care; in return bees deliver products (honey, wax, propolis) and essential pollination for food and wild plants. Beyond honey bees (Apis mellifera and A. cerana), apiculture increasingly includes bumble bees for greenhouse crops, stingless bees (meliponiculture) in the tropics, and solitary bees for orchard pollination.

Good apiculture is practical and evidence-based: monitor colony health, manage pests and diseases, support forage, and work with local regulations. Whether you’re a hobbyist with two hives or a commercial operator with thousands, the principles are the same: healthy bees, adequate forage, and responsible stewardship.
