Colony losses in 2025 — what COLOSS research means for your bees

23 September 2025 #beehealth #colonyloss #COLOSS

Colony losses are never just “one thing”. COLOSS surveys and partner studies keep pointing to a familiar gang: varroa and viruses, queen issues, nutrition gaps, and weather/forage swings. The good news? There’s loads you can do — and most of it is simple, planned, and repeatable.

Four big drivers (in plain English)

  • Varroa + viruses: If mites run the show, viruses do the damage. Monitor, rotate treatments, and don’t leave “just one more week”.
  • Queens: Failing or poorly mated queens mean dwindly colonies. Know your queen’s age, have a spare plan, and requeen before it’s a drama.
  • Nutrition: Sparse forage or long nectar gaps knock brood rearing. Think pollen, think timing, and keep an eye on stores.
  • Weather & forage: Hot dry spring? Rain-lashed summer? Expect odd flows and be ready to adapt inspections and feeding.

Three quick wins for hobbyists

  1. Make varroa visible: Sugar roll or alcohol wash as standard — pop it in your diary. Treat to thresholds, not vibes.
  2. Track your queens: A simple card or app noting age, temperament, and brood pattern helps you act early.
  3. Feed with intent: If forage is patchy, top up — and don’t ignore protein. A tiny nudge at the right time beats a big rescue later.

And three for commercial operators

  1. Standardise mite management: Apiary-level monitoring, rotation across seasons, and resistance-aware choices.
  2. Queen pipeline: Line-bred stock, clear replacement windows, and nucleus capacity to swap fast.
  3. Data > guesswork: Weather + yield + disease logs. Spot patterns, move early, reduce losses.

What to watch next

COLOSS will keep publishing multi-country results — useful for spotting regional patterns and odd seasons. Pair that with your own notes and you’ll get a feel for when to feed, split, or sit tight.
