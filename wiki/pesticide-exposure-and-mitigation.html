Pesticide exposure & mitigation

Bees can encounter pesticides via treated crops, contaminated water, or drift. While lethal events are obvious, sublethal effects (navigation, immunity, brood care) also matter.

How exposure happens

  • Foraging on treated flowering crops or adjacent weeds.
  • Dust/drift during seed drilling or spraying.
  • Contaminated puddles, gutters, or irrigation water.

Risk reduction

  • Build relationships with local farmers; coordinate spray times outside flight hours.
  • Place hives with buffer zones from high-risk crops where possible.
  • Provide clean water sources at the apiary.
  • Maintain strong colonies; stress makes effects worse.

After an incident

  • Document with photos and samples; contact local authorities per guidance.
  • Retain affected comb/bee samples for testing.
  • Update your records and review placement strategy.
