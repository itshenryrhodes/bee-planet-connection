Hygienic behaviour & breeding

Some colonies naturally detect and remove diseased or mite-infested brood. This hygienic behaviour slows the spread of brood diseases and reduces Varroa reproduction.

Traits and benefits

  • Uncapping and removal: Workers identify abnormal brood and remove it quickly.
  • Disease resistance: Helps limit foulbroods and viruses.
  • Varroa control: Mites reproducing in capped cells are disrupted.

Testing hygienic behaviour

  • Freeze-killed brood assay: Patch of brood is frozen; hygienic colonies clear it rapidly.
  • Pin-killed brood test: Similar principle with punctured brood.

Breeding for resilience

  • Select queens from colonies that show strong hygiene and survivorship.
  • Use local mating stations or controlled insemination for consistency.
  • Balance traits — hygiene is valuable, but temperament and productivity also matter.

VSH & SMR traits

Varroa Sensitive Hygiene (VSH) and Suppressed Mite Reproduction (SMR) lines are breeding programmes that enhance natural varroa resistance.
