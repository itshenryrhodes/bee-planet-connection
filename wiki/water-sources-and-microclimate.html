Water sources & microclimate

Bees need water for cooling, brood food, and dissolving crystallised stores. Providing a clean source and managing the apiary’s microclimate reduce stress during heatwaves and dry spells.

Setting up a reliable water source

  • Shallow with landing: Use pebbles, corks or floating mats so bees don’t drown.
  • Consistent place: Bees learn locations; keep it topped up in the same spot.
  • Clean but not sterile: A touch of mineral content helps; avoid algae blooms.
  • Buffer against pesticides: A good water source reduces risk of bees visiting contaminated puddles (see pesticide exposure).

Shade, airflow and orientation

  • Give hives midday/afternoon shade in hot climates; morning sun helps early flight.
  • Ensure ventilation so colonies can regulate temperature/humidity.
  • Face entrances away from prevailing winds to reduce drafts.

Heat stress signs

  • Bearding (masses of bees on the front of the hive).
  • Excessive fanning and reduced foraging.
  • Warped comb or overheated brood if prolonged.

Quick mitigations

  • Add shade cloth or temporary covers during heatwaves.
  • Increase entrance/roof ventilation; consider mesh floors.
  • Ensure water is within a few metres of the apiary.
