Caste system & lifecycle

Honey bee colonies have three castes: queen, workers and drones. Their roles and development times drive seasonal management, from swarm control to requeening.

Development timeline (days)

CasteEggLarvaPupaTotal
Queen3~5~8~16
Worker3~6~12~21
Drone3~7~14~24

Roles

  • Queen: Primary egg-layer; her pheromones stabilise colony behaviour.
  • Workers: Female bees with age-related tasks (nurses → builders → foragers).
  • Drones: Male bees that mate with queens; no foraging or stinging.

Lifecycle & management

  • Spring build-up: Expanding brood nest; ensure feed and space.
  • Swarm season: Queen cells appear; apply control methods.
  • Late season: Focus on healthy winter bees; manage Varroa.
