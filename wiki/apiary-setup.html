Apiary setup & siting

Where and how you set up hives has lasting effects on colony health, honey yields, and neighbour relations. A good site balances forage access, microclimate, and biosecurity.

Site essentials

  • Forage: Access to diverse plants (see forage by season).
  • Water: Provide or ensure a clean water source.
  • Sun/shade: Morning sun is ideal; avoid full-day exposure in hot climates.
  • Drainage: Site on well-drained ground to reduce damp-related disease.

Hive stands & spacing

  • Elevate hives to reduce damp and back strain.
  • Space entrances to limit drifting and disease spread.
  • Use landmarks or varied orientation to help bee navigation.

Access & neighbours

  • Ensure safe vehicle access for moving supers and equipment.
  • Keep hives discreet and flight paths directed away from footpaths/houses.

Biosecurity considerations

Good siting and separation help prevent cross-contamination of brood diseases and pests like Varroa.
