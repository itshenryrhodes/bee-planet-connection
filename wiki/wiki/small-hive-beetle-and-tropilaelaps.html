Small hive beetle & Tropilaelaps

Two exotic pests cause concern globally: the small hive beetle (SHB) and Tropilaelaps mites. Neither is established in the UK, but both are on the watch list.

Small hive beetle (Aethina tumida)

  • Native to sub-Saharan Africa; spread to USA, Australia, parts of Europe.
  • Beetle larvae tunnel through comb, defecating and causing honey to ferment (“slime”).
  • Adult beetles hide in hives; hard to spot without traps.
  • Control: good hive hygiene, traps, strong colonies, and containment protocols in affected areas.

Tropilaelaps mites

  • Originally parasites of Asian giant honey bees; now spillover into A. mellifera.
  • Reproduce faster than Varroa, causing rapid colony collapse.
  • Do not survive long without brood — brood breaks can help, but biosecurity is key.

Biosecurity & monitoring

  • Import controls and inspections are essential to prevent spread.
  • Beekeepers should stay updated on notifiable pest alerts from regulators.
