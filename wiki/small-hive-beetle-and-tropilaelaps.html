Small hive beetle & Tropilaelaps

Two invasive pests cause global concern for apiculture: the small hive beetle (Aethina tumida) and Tropilaelaps mites. Both spread quickly and damage colonies severely.

Small hive beetle (SHB)

  • Native to Africa; spread to North America, Australia, southern Europe.
  • Larvae tunnel through comb, ruin honey stores, cause slime and fermentation.
  • Adults hide in crevices; difficult to spot in early stages.

Tropilaelaps mites

  • Native to Asia; parasitise brood similar to Varroa.
  • Reproduce faster than Varroa; colonies collapse quickly when infested.
  • Cannot survive long without brood — brood breaks are one control option.

Management

  • Good biosecurity: avoid importing uninspected bees/equipment.
  • Regular inspections for signs of beetle larvae or Tropilaelaps damage.
  • In affected regions: traps, soil drenches (SHB), brood interruption (Tropilaelaps).
  • Report suspected finds — both are notifiable in many countries.
