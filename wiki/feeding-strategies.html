Feeding strategies

Strategic feeding keeps colonies resilient through forage gaps and helps prepare them for winter. Use it to support — not replace — natural forage.

When to feed

  • Spring build-up: Light syrup to stimulate activity if natural nectar is scarce.
  • Summer dearths: Maintain strength during gaps; avoid triggering robbing.
  • Autumn: Heavy syrup to build winter stores after the last harvest.
  • Winter emergency: Fondant/candy when stores run low.

Syrup ratios & formats

TypeRatio (sugar:water)Use
Light syrup1:1 by weightSpring stimulation
Heavy syrup2:1 by weightAutumn stores
Fondant/candySolid feedWinter emergency/top-up

Protein (pollen) supplements

Use only when natural pollen is lacking. Overuse can mask problems. Monitor brood pattern and colony condition. See planting guides to improve local forage.

Feeders & placement

  • Contact feeders and top feeders reduce robbing risk.
  • Avoid open feeding; it spreads disease and invites robbing.
  • Keep equipment clean to support hygiene.

Do’s & don’ts

  • Use white granulated sugar; avoid brown sugar or molasses.
  • Do not feed syrup while honey supers are on for human consumption.
  • Record feeds in your inspection notes.
