Hive Insulation and Ventilation

Bees manage temperature well, but poor hive insulation or airflow leads to condensation and stress. Aim for a warm, dry brood nest with controlled moisture escape.

Key points

  • Top insulation reduces heat loss and condensation under the roof.
  • Small upper vents or breathable quilts help moisture exit.
  • Solid floors can be fine if moisture is managed; mesh floors add airflow.

Practical tips

  • Use insulated crownboards or quilts in winter.
  • Maintain a slight forward tilt for water run-off.
  • Avoid large draughts; bees prefer stable microclimates.
