Ethics & welfare

Bee welfare debates touch on stocking density, manipulation, and the ethics of breeding for docility vs productivity.

Key concerns

  • Overstocking: Strains forage and stresses colonies.
  • Manipulation: Frequent disturbance may increase disease risk.
  • Breeding: Selecting only for honey yield may weaken resilience.

Balancing welfare

Many natural beekeepers draw inspiration from traditional hives and focus on reduced intervention.
