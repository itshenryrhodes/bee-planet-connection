Hive types: National, Langstroth, Dadant, Flow & top-bar

Modern beekeeping uses modular hives that let you expand and inspect easily. Choosing the right pattern depends on local availability, your back strength, and what your beekeeping mates use (spare frames at short notice are gold). Here’s a practical overview of the most common boxes.

Quick comparison

HiveWhere commonFrames/foundationProsWatch-outs
National UK & Ireland Standard National frames; 14×12 variant for deeper brood Easy to source; lighter boxes; lots of local knowledge Frame sizes differ from Langstroth (compatibility issues)
Langstroth Worldwide Deep/medium/shallow frames; global standardisation Great availability; scalable; widely supported equipment Full deeps can be heavy; choose mediums if weight is a concern
Dadant Europe, parts of N. America Large brood frames; shallower supers Spacious brood nest; fewer brood frames to inspect Heavy components; compatibility mostly within Dadant systems
Flow Global (hobbyists) Special Flow frames in supers; standard brood below Convenient honey harvesting from supers Cost; still need regular inspections and swarm management
Top-bar Global (low-tech/educational) Bars rather than frames (foundationless) Simpler carpentry; gentle handling; great for education More cross-comb risk; harder to scale for large honey crops

Picking a hive you’ll actually enjoy using

  • Local standard: Matching your local beekeepers makes life easier for spares and advice.
  • Lift limits: If weight is a worry, consider medium supers or poly boxes.
  • Honey goals: Chasing big crops? Go modular and scalable (Langstroth/National).
  • Budget & DIY: Top-bar and some National setups are friendlier for home builders.

Core components you’ll meet

  • Brood box: Where the queen lays and brood develops.
  • Supers: Honey storage for harvest; added during flows.
  • Frames & foundation: Wooden/plastic frames; wax or plastic foundation to guide comb.
  • Queen excluder: Keeps queen in the brood area to avoid brood in honey supers.
  • Floor/stand, crownboard/inner cover, roof: Ventilation and weatherproofing components.
