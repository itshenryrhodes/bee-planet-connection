Requeening Calendar

Planned requeening improves genetics and keeps colonies productive. Timing varies with climate; the aim is to ensure strong laying before major flows.

Seasonal guidance

  • Spring: Replace poor queens early; build for main nectar flow.
  • Summer: Post-harvest requeening reduces swarming next year.
  • Autumn: Requeen only with proven mated queens to ensure winter viability.

Signals to requeen

  • Spotty brood pattern, increased defensiveness, frequent supersedure cells.
  • Age > 2 years in production hives.
