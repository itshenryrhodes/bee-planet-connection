Planting guides & seed mixes

Beekeepers and gardeners can do much to support forage continuity by planting bee-friendly species. The goal is to cover early spring through late autumn with overlapping nectar and pollen sources.

Recommended seed mixes (temperate zones)

Season focusKey plants
Spring boost Phacelia, crimson clover, borage, early flowering brassicas
Summer nectar White/red clover, sunflower, sainfoin, buckwheat, lavender
Late forage Borage (late sowings), mustard, goldenrod, late asters, ivy (wild)

Habitat layers

  • Trees: Lime, chestnut, willow, apple, cherry.
  • Shrubs: Cotoneaster, mahonia, rosemary.
  • Herbs: Thyme, oregano, mint, borage.
  • Annuals/perennials: Phacelia, clovers, sedum.

Tips for planting success

  • Mix annuals and perennials for immediate and lasting impact.
  • Sow in staggered batches to extend flowering periods.
  • Choose native species where possible for resilience and biodiversity.
  • Ensure water availability and reduce pesticide use nearby.
