Queen health & requeening

A vigorous queen underpins colony performance. Spotting queen issues early prevents cascading problems with brood pattern, temperament and swarming.

Signs of queen problems

  • Patchy brood pattern despite good stores and weather.
  • Frequent supersedure cells outside swarming season.
  • Hot/defensive behaviour emerging in a previously calm colony.
  • Drone-laying queen or laying workers (multiple eggs per cell).

Requeening options

  • Introduce a mated queen: Quick reset; choose lines with hygienic traits.
  • Combine colonies: Newspaper method to unite a weak queenless unit with a strong queenright colony.
  • Raise your own: Grafting or using strong queen cells from good colonies.

Introduction tips

  • Ensure colony is queenless for 24–48 hours before introduction.
  • Introduce in a cage; allow workers to chew through candy over 2–3 days.
  • Avoid nectar dearth introductions (higher rejection); light feeding can help.

Track outcomes

Note dates and results in records. Evaluate brood pattern and temperament over 2–3 weeks.
