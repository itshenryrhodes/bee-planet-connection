Bee flight mechanics

Honey bee flight has fascinated scientists for decades due to its unusual aerodynamics.

Wing movement

  • Bees beat wings about 200–250 times per second.
  • Asynchronous flight muscles allow high frequency without nerve fatigue.
  • Forewings and hindwings hook together via hamuli.

Aerodynamics

  • Bees generate lift through rapid wing reversal and leading-edge vortices.
  • Efficient even with short, stubby wings relative to body size.

Energy

  • Flight is energy-intensive; nectar fuels wing muscles directly.
  • Foraging ranges are typically 2–5 km from the hive.
