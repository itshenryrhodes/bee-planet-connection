Inspections & record-keeping

Regular inspections are the backbone of good beekeeping. They reveal colony strength, health issues, and whether interventions are needed. Written records help spot trends and support management decisions.

Inspection checklist

What to checkWhy it matters
Queen presence / eggsConfirms colony continuity
Brood patternSolid pattern = healthy queen; patchy = disease or failing queen
Stores (nectar/pollen)Ensure enough food to support brood
Swarming signsQueen cells, congestion, drones
Pests/diseasesVarroa levels, brood disease symptoms

Frequency

  • Spring/summer build-up: every 7–10 days.
  • Late summer: reduce frequency once swarming risk passes.
  • Autumn/winter: minimal disturbance; heft to gauge stores.

Record-keeping tips

  • Note date, weather, colony ID, findings, and actions.
  • Simple coded sheets (✓ queen seen, ✓ eggs present, etc.) save time.
  • Digital tools and apps exist, but paper in the bee suit pocket still works.

Why records matter

Records build a picture over time: which colonies are productive, which overwinter well, which are disease-resistant.
