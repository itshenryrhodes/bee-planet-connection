Stress & heat management

High temperatures, poor ventilation and forage dearths increase colony stress, making bees more susceptible to viruses and disease. Proactive steps keep colonies stable through extremes.

Recognising stress

  • Persistent bearding and heavy fanning at the entrance.
  • Reduced brood area despite queen presence and stores.
  • Robbing behaviour in dearths; defensive temperament shifts.

Cooling & ventilation

  • Provide shade during peak sun; use breathable roofs or vented crownboards.
  • Ensure mesh floors and adequate entrance size; add upper vents if condensation forms.
  • Keep supers appropriately sized — too much empty space can hinder thermoregulation.

Nutrition under heat

  • Top up with light syrup during severe dearths (no honey supers fitted).
  • Support continuous forage with plantings.
  • Maintain a close water source.

Operations & timing

  • Schedule heavy manipulations for cooler parts of the day.
  • Avoid prolonged hive opening during heatwaves.
  • Consider temporary entrance reducers to deter robbing.
