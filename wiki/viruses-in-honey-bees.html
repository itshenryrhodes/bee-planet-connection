Viruses in honey bees

Honey bees host a range of viruses. Most circulate at low levels but can flare into disease under stress, particularly with Varroa mite infestations that act as amplifiers and vectors.

Key viruses

  • Deformed wing virus (DWV): Causes characteristic wing deformities; closely linked with Varroa transmission.
  • Chronic bee paralysis virus (CBPV): Causes trembling, hairless black bees, and colony collapse in severe cases.
  • Israeli acute paralysis virus (IAPV): Implicated in colony losses in some regions; transmitted by mites.
  • Black queen cell virus, Sacbrood virus: Affect brood; usually subclinical unless combined with other stresses.

Transmission routes

  • Varroa feeding and vectoring.
  • Oral/fecal routes within the colony.
  • Contaminated equipment and drifting/robbing bees.

Management

  • Keep Varroa levels low — main control lever.
  • Support strong, well-fed colonies.
  • Replace queens to refresh colony genetics.
  • Practice good biosecurity (clean tools, avoid robbing).

Research outlook

Molecular studies continue to reveal complex virus interactions. Many viruses are widespread, so management focuses on reducing stressors and transmission opportunities rather than eradication.
