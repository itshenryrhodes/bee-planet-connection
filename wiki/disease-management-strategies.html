Disease management strategies

No single tool solves all bee health issues. The most effective approach is an integrated strategy combining prevention, monitoring, and intervention.

Prevention

  • Keep colonies strong and well-fed.
  • Practice good hygiene and source bees from reputable suppliers.
  • Rotate comb and avoid old, contaminated equipment.

Monitoring

  • Inspect brood regularly for signs of disease.
  • Check adult bees for symptoms (e.g. paralysis, dysentery).
  • Measure Varroa levels using sugar roll or alcohol wash.

Intervention

  • Apply authorised treatments when thresholds are exceeded.
  • Requeen weak or diseased colonies with healthy stock.
  • Destroy colonies with notifiable brood diseases as required by law.

Resilient beekeeping

Combining hygienic breeding, forage diversity, and careful stock management builds colonies better able to withstand viruses, bacteria and pests.
