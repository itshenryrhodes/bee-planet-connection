Queen rearing & grafting

Raising your own queens improves local adaptation and biosecurity. This guide covers tools, timing and colony setup.

Approaches

  • Emergency cells: From queenless colonies (variable quality).
  • Grafting: Transfer ≤24-hour larvae to cups; use starter/finisher system.
  • Cupkit/ Jenter: Egg laying into cups avoids grafting.

Starter & finisher

  • Starter: Queenless, crowded with young bees and feed.
  • Finisher: Queenright above excluder; strong nurse population.

Timeline (days)

  • D0 graft; D5–6 cap; D10–11 move cells; D16 emergence (protect from chilling).

Mating nucs

  • Use 2–5 frame nucs; ensure water and feed.
  • Place near sites with good weather windows.
