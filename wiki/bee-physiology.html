Bee physiology

Honey bees have specialised anatomy and physiology enabling efficient foraging, communication and survival.

Key systems

  • Digestive system: Crop (“honey stomach”) for nectar transport.
  • Sensory adaptations: UV vision, polarised light navigation.
  • Flight muscles: Asynchronous wing beats enable hovering and long foraging flights.

Defence

Stinger and venom apparatus evolved from ovipositors; barbed in workers, smooth in queens.
