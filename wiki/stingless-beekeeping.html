Stingless beekeeping (meliponiculture)

Stingless bees (tribe Meliponini) are tropical social bees managed for honey, wax, and pollination services.

Distribution

  • Mainly in Central and South America, Africa, Southeast Asia, and Australia.
  • Over 500 described species.

Products

  • Honey (meliponine honey): Tangy flavour, high medicinal value, smaller yields.
  • Wax: Softer than honey bee wax, used locally in crafts.

Beekeeping methods

  • Log hives and box hives adapted for species.
  • Colonies are less defensive than Apis mellifera.
  • Important for indigenous livelihoods and agroforestry systems.
