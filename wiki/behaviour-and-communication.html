Behaviour & communication

Honey bees coordinate colony life through pheromones, vibration and the famous waggle dance. Understanding behaviour helps with swarm prevention, gentle handling, and interpreting colony needs.

Pheromones

  • Queen pheromones: Maintain social cohesion and suppress worker ovary development.
  • Nasonov (orientation) pheromone: Guides returning workers to the colony.
  • Alarm pheromones: Trigger defensive responses; smoke masks these during inspections.

Waggle dance & recruitment

Foragers advertise profitable sources by dancing, encoding direction and distance relative to the sun’s position.

Thermoregulation

Bees fan, evaporate water, and cluster to keep brood near 35°C; see microclimate and ventilation.

Defensive behaviour

  • Genetics, weather and nectar flow influence colony mood.
  • Requeen persistently aggressive colonies (queen health).
