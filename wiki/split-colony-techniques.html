Split Colony Techniques

Splitting colonies controls swarming, breaks brood cycles for mite management, and increases apiary numbers. Choose timing that aligns with nectar flow and local weather.

Approaches

  • Walk-away split: Divide brood and resources; one side raises a queen.
  • Controlled split: Introduce a mated queen to the queenless half.
  • Making nucs: 2–5 frames with food, brood, and bees to start a new colony.

Key facts

  • Ensure both halves have eggs/young larvae or a new queen.
  • Provide adequate food stores to avoid setbacks.
  • Use a queen excluder to find the queen before splitting if needed.

Practical tips

  • Mark frames so each split gets open brood and capped brood.
  • Equalise bees at dusk to balance foragers.
  • Close entrances for transport and to prevent drifting.
