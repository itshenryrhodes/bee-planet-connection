Forage ecology

Bees depend on diverse floral landscapes. Forage ecology explores plant–pollinator networks, seasonal shifts, and competition between managed and wild pollinators.

Key factors

  • Bloom sequence: Continuous forage avoids gaps in nectar/pollen supply.
  • Competition: Dense apiaries may stress local resources for wild bees.
  • Landscape diversity: Mixed farmland, hedgerows and wildflowers support resilience.

Management implications

Planting forage mixes, limiting stocking density, and collaborating with farmers improve outcomes for honey bees and biodiversity.
