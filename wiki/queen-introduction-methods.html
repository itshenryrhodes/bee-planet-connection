Queen Introduction Methods

Introducing a new queen can refresh genetics, temper colony behaviour, and restore brood after a queen loss. Success depends on colony condition, timing, and scent integration.

Key facts

  • Best results when the colony is queenless for 12–24 hours.
  • Use slow-release methods (e.g., candy plug) to allow pheromone acceptance.
  • Reduce stressors: avoid robbing pressure and keep entrances small.

Common methods

  • Caged, candy plug: Hang between brood frames; workers chew through in 2–3 days.
  • Nuc acclimatisation: Introduce in a nuc first, then combine.
  • Newspaper combine: For merging a queenright nuc with a queenless hive.

Practical tips

  • Check for queen cells and destroy before introduction.
  • Do not disturb for 5–7 days; then verify eggs/young larvae.
  • Feed light syrup to lower defensive behaviour during the change.
