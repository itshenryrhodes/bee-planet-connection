Swarm control

Swarming is natural reproduction, but unmanaged swarms reduce honey yield and risk spreading Varroa and disease. Control balances bee instincts with beekeeper goals.

Why swarming happens

  • Congestion in the brood nest.
  • Old or failing queen.
  • Seasonal instinct at peak nectar flows.

Signs of preparation

  • Queen cells on frame bottoms.
  • Nectar backfilling brood area.
  • Reduced laying from the queen.

Control methods

  • Artificial swarm: Move queen + flyers to a new box.
  • Demaree method: Separate brood from queen with supers in between.
  • Splitting: Make nucleus colonies from strong hives.
  • Requeening: Replace ageing queens early (see queen health).

If a colony swarms

Assess brood, manage queen cells, prevent further casts. Keep notes in inspection records.

Health link

Frequent unmanaged swarms spread viruses and brood disease via drifting/robbing.
