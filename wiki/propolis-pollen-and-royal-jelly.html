Propolis, pollen & royal jelly

Beyond honey and wax, bees produce propolis, pollen stores, and royal jelly — each with nutritional and commercial significance.

Propolis

Resin collected from buds and sap flows; used to seal gaps, line brood cells, and as an antimicrobial.

Pollen

Collected in corbiculae; stored as “bee bread” with enzymes. Vital protein for brood rearing.

Royal jelly

Produced by nurse bees’ glands; fed to all larvae briefly, and continuously to queens.

Uses & trade

  • Propolis: tinctures, supplements, varnishes.
  • Pollen: supplements, livestock feed additives.
  • Royal jelly: niche health products, queen rearing.
