Extraction, filtration & cream honey

Harvesting honey is one of the great rewards of beekeeping. Careful extraction and handling preserve quality, reduce contamination, and keep honey attractive to consumers.

Preparation

  • Only extract from capped combs to ensure honey is ripe (moisture < 20%).
  • Warm supers to room temperature to ease spinning.
  • Ensure all equipment is clean, dry and food-grade.

Extraction methods

  • Radial or tangential extractors: Centrifugal spinning; radial is more efficient for hobbyists.
  • Crush and strain: Simple, no extractor needed; yields more wax.
  • Flow hives: Honey flows directly from special frames (still requires post-handling).

Filtration & settling

Strain honey through food-grade nylon or stainless steel sieves to remove wax and debris. Allow to settle for 24–48 hours in a ripener to let air bubbles rise before bottling.

Cream honey (soft-set)

Cream honey is deliberately crystallised to a smooth, spreadable texture:

  • Start with fine-crystal “seed” honey.
  • Mix thoroughly into liquid honey (10–20%).
  • Store at cool temperature (14°C) to allow uniform crystallisation.

Hygiene & safety

  • Food-grade containers only.
  • Avoid overheating (>40°C), which damages enzymes and aroma.
  • Label correctly with country-of-origin and batch/date as required.
