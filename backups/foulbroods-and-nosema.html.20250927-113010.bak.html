Foulbroods & Nosema

Bacterial brood diseases and gut parasites can cause major colony losses. Understanding the symptoms and following control protocols is essential for biosecurity.

American foulbrood (AFB)

Caused by Paenibacillus larvae. Brood becomes discoloured, sunken and ropey. AFB spores are highly persistent, making destruction of affected colonies a legal requirement in many countries.

European foulbrood (EFB)

Caused by Melissococcus plutonius. Affects unsealed larvae; brood appears twisted or melted, often with sour odour. Outcomes vary: some colonies recover with management, others need comb destruction.

Both AFB and EFB are notifiable diseases in the UK and many other jurisdictions. Suspected cases must be reported to the bee inspectorate.

Nosema

Nosema spp. are microsporidian parasites of the adult bee gut. N. apis (traditional) and N. ceranae (more widespread today) can reduce longevity, weaken colonies, and increase susceptibility to other stressors.

  • Symptoms: Dysentery, dwindling colonies, poor spring build-up.
  • Diagnosis: Microscopy of bee gut samples; PCR in labs.
  • Management: Replacing comb, ensuring good forage and nutrition, selecting resilient stock.

Prevention & management

  • Maintain good apiary hygiene (clean tools, avoid comb exchange between colonies).
  • Replace old comb regularly to limit pathogen build-up.
  • Source queens and bees from trusted, disease-free apiaries.
