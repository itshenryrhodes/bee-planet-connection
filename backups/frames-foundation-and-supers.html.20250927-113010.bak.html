Frames, foundation & supers

Frames hold comb straight for inspection and harvest. Foundation guides comb building, and supers are added above the brood box to store surplus honey. Choices affect swarm risk, inspection speed and honey quality.

Foundation options

  • Wax foundation: Traditional, bees draw readily; can recycle cappings into sheets.
  • Plastic foundation: Durable; coat with wax to encourage drawing.
  • Foundationless: Guides or starter strips yield natural comb; slower and needs careful handling.

Supering strategy

  • Add supers ahead of strong nectar flows to reduce congestion.
  • Use a queen excluder to keep brood out of honey supers.
  • During heavy flows, have spare supers ready; store frames dry and pest-free.

Practical tips

  • Maintain bee space to avoid brace comb.
  • Rotate dark brood comb out over time for hygiene (biosecurity).
  • Record frame/foundation changes in records.
