Brood diseases overview

Several pathogens affect developing larvae and pupae. Recognising symptoms early and applying correct responses prevents wider colony and apiary losses.

Summary table

DiseaseCauseSymptomsNotes
American foulbrood (AFB) Paenibacillus larvae Ropey larval remains, sunken cappings Notifiable; spores persist for decades
European foulbrood (EFB) Melissococcus plutonius Twisted larvae, patchy brood, sour smell Notifiable; some colonies self-clear
Sacbrood virus Viral Larvae in fluid-filled sacs, fail to pupate Often clears without intervention
Chalkbrood Ascosphaera apis (fungus) Mummified white/grey larvae Linked to stress, damp conditions

Management principles

  • Report suspected AFB/EFB immediately.
  • Maintain good biosecurity.
  • Requeen with hygienic stock if brood diseases recur.
  • Keep hives well-ventilated and avoid dampness.
