Honey Authenticity Guide

Honey authenticity is a major concern for consumers, regulators, and producers. Fraudulent practices such as adulteration with sugar syrups or mislabelling of floral and geographic origin undermine trust and reduce the value of genuine honey.

Key Methods for Testing Authenticity

  • Physical and chemical analysis — moisture content, HMF levels, electrical conductivity.
  • Spectroscopic methods — FTIR, NMR, and isotope analysis for detecting adulterants.
  • Pollen analysis (melissopalynology) — determining botanical and geographical origin.
  • DNA-based methods — barcoding and sequencing to identify plant sources.

Global Standards

International frameworks such as the Codex Alimentarius and EU honey directives set benchmarks for quality, labelling, and authenticity testing.

Strengthening transparency across the supply chain — from beekeeper to retailer — helps ensure consumers receive authentic, high-quality honey while protecting the livelihoods of honest producers.
